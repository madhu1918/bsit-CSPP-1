According to a legend it was ruled or controlled by a Cooch-Behar king, Sangaldip, around the 7th century BC but not much is known prior to the introduction of Tibet Buddisht in the 9th century, when turmoil in Tibetforced many monks to flee to Bhutan. In the 12th century, the Drukpa Kagyupa school was established and remains the dominant form of Buddhism in Bhutan today. The country's political history is intimately tied to its religious history and relations among the various monastic schools and monasteries.
